What to say when you call

  • “I have a rabbit emergency — do you treat rabbits / exotics?”
  • State the main sign (not eating, collapsed, bloated, flystrike) and how long.
  • Ask whether they have a rabbit-experienced vet on now, or where to go if not.
  • Confirm address, opening hours, and whether to come straight in.

Prepare before you go

  • Note the last time your rabbit ate, drank, and passed droppings.
  • Bring a list of foods, treats, and any medications.
  • Transport your rabbit warm, secure, and with familiar-smelling bedding.
  • Bring a companion bunny if bonded, to reduce stress, if advised.

When it's an emergency

Treat not eating with no droppings, collapse, bloat, flystrike, severe head tilt, or laboured breathing as a go-now emergency. See our gut stasis, heatstroke, and flystrike guides for the warning signs.
